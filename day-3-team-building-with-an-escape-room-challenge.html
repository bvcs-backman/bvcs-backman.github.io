Day 3: Team building with an escape room challenge

Posted on January 17, 2019 by Nathan Backman

[Written by Kayle & Michael]

The main touristy thing we did today was attempt to complete an escape room in downtown Seattle. This was the first time some of us had attempted an escape room, including myself (Kayle). This particular one had a back-and-forth sort of gimmick, embellished with flashing LED lights, where clues existed in one room and you’d need to relay them to the other with notes. This was the biggest time sink, often running from the first to the second and forgetting everything in between.

As with many escape rooms, you have one hour to complete the challenge. Towards the end we made good progress, but were probably 5 to 10 minutes from finishing the entire puzzle. It was interesting and something we would like to try again in the future. The solve rate was around 28%, though some in the group have solved lower. After our time in the escape room was up we asked about the functionality of the LED lights, more specifically how they worked. They told us that it was all done using stage light software, so it was similar to something that you’d see at a live concert.

   

Right before the escape room we went out to eat at Al Basha. This was a Mediterranean style restaurant serving lamb chops to gyros to shawarma and everything in between. Most of the group thought their dishes were great, while others had a less enjoyable time. It seemed to be hit or miss, but if you want to hear how we really felt, you’ll have to check out our app.

   

As for app development during this day, the main focus was on the beginning of transitioning to a proper error-handling system. The goal was to have it finished by the end of the day, but it rolled into the next day. Some of the UI team worked on small tweaks to make it look better. On the back-end, we made a small change to allow emails with capitals or leading/trailing spaces.
