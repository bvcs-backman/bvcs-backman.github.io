Day 7: Excursion to Portland and Knuckleheads Inc.

Posted on January 18, 2019 by Nathan Backman

[Written by Michael & Nick]

On this day, we all put coding aside to take a trip down to Portland Oregon. It was a little odd for all of us to just abandon our PC’s after so many days of development, but everybody thought the trip would deem to be an invaluable experience. Luckily nobody got car sick on the way down, and a HUGE shout out to Dr. Bezuglov for driving the whole way down! When we arrived in Portland, the first thing we did was grab some local pizza. We stopped by Sizzle Pie for a couple hot slices, however none of us were extremely impressed by the chain. After all, how could you top Cassey’s!?


           
Immediately after, we took a short jog over to stop at Powell’s City of Books. This is the largest independent book store in the world, covering and entire city block and  every genre of book ten times over. We were all drawn upstairs to the rare books section and of course the computer science section. They had books on display for tens of thousands of dollars and that wasn’t even their most expensive books which rang in at three hundred thousand!

      

After about an hour of book browsing we made our way over to Knuckleheads Inc. to meet up with Bryan, Dr. Backman’s college friend. I (Michael) volunteered to pitch our application to the professionals there (a mistake which opted me for that role from then on). We gained valuable information from debugging to time management and everything in between to take back with us.

When Dr. Backman finished catching up with his Whitworth University buddy, the lot of us made our way over to VooDoo Doughnuts and then Ground Kontrol, a classic video game arcade. I can’t think of any other way to explain VooDoo Doughnuts other than with a quote from Dr. Backman: “VooDoo Doughnuts is like the zombie burger of pastries.”

      

Unfortunately we didn’t get to spend a ton of time in the arcade, however the time we did get was not wasted. They had almost every quarter arcade machine you could imagine there, and all of us were graced with Drew’s amazing, awe-inducing Street Fighter prowess.

      

Eventually the time came for us to head out to HUB (Hopworks Urban Brewery). Bryan met back up with us for dinner while also getting to meet Chris, another one of Dr. Backman’s Whitworth buddies. We spent a lot of time talking to these two about career advice, advice about our app, and a multitude of other extremely valuable information that none of us took for granted.

      

After dinner ended and Bryan and Chris made their exit, it was back to Vancouver, WA where we spent the night in a hotel and prepared ourselves for an early morning the next day.
