Day 16: Microsoft and escape room redemption

Posted on January 24, 2019 by Nathan Backman

[Written by Michael & Garrett]

There wasn’t too much coding that went into today because of a packed day involving Microsoft and a second try at an escape room. What we did do happened in the morning where Drew, Tyler, and Kayle worked to benefit the users ease of access with a few UI fixes. Drew added context for the rating sliders, Tyler updated the review page and Kayle added auto complete and spell check.

      

After the small coding session, we headed out to Café 65 where we met up with Mike, a BVU alum that’s been working on Microsoft’s SQL server since 1991 when they had only 20 people on their team. For a bit of context the team is now over 1200 strong and still growing.

      

The whole team had a great time meeting Mike over lunch even though a couple of us tempted fate a little too much when we ordered our dishes with a little too much spice. Mike invited us over to tour the Microsoft office and after we managed to cool down we all made our way over to the campus.

      

Upon arrival, we checked in with Mike and followed him around the campus. He told us that, previously, Microsoft had individual offices for all the employees but recently reconfigured to an open layout to encourage collaboration and it really showed. The offices spaces were large and open, filled with all the tech (and pens) you would ever need to be successful.

  

We had a good chat with Mike getting valuable information about being successful in any type of job that you want to go into (and even his opinions on NoSQL). After our chat, he directed us over to building 92 which had a Microsoft store and museum as well as demo space inside.

      

All of us started off in the Microsoft store, testing out all their new technology. Most of us were drawn to their Microsoft surface book where we showed off our not-at-all-impressive 3D art skills. After we were done making fools of ourselves in the store we went across the hallway to the museum which had memorabilia throughout Microsoft’s lifetime. It was really cool to see the history of the Microsoft throughout the ages. Many of us were also drawn to the back of the museum where they had gadgets to play with.

  

Initially we were all drawn to facial recognition computers which would either guess your age, what dog breed you were, what your emotion was, or what celebrity you were. The majority of us found this to be fun and interesting even though the age guesser was usually way over our actual ages.

      

 

From there we split up to check out the other parts of the museum. Kayle and Nick hopped on their display Xbox One to drift around Forza. I (Michael) played around the display phone which had been equipped to recognize text and objects through the camera. Drew and Dr. Backman went to go play intense games of halo on their pro gaming setup, and I (Garrett) found my way over to the force-feedback racing chair, set up with four pistons to imitate how it would feel if you were actually driving (and it did a really good job at that).

      
Once we were done with the museum Mike met back up with us to guide us back to our vehicle and after a picture with him we said our goodbyes and went on our way.

  

Once back at the house we had a bit of down time before we went back out into downtown Seattle for a bite to eat. Because we misjudged time by a bit and because I (Michael) led the team on a wild goose chase for a restaurant that didn’t exist (curse you the Pink Door) we had to just grab a snack at the first Starbucks before hitting up the escape room.

  

We opted for escaping the lost temple this time, a slightly easier room, but one that had a lot of care put into it because it was one of their first ones. This time we killed it, escaping in forty three minutes which left us with a ton of time to spare. The person leading our room told us that we were the fastest group she ever had (not the fastest time ever for the room) and also explained to us how we were at the first ever escape room in the United States, a fact our first guide seemed to gloss over.

      

Post-escape room, we wandered around hunting for food. We looked at several places finally settling on the Yard House. Drew and I (Garrett) split a Carnivore Pizza which we were misled as to the size. By the time we left it was about 10:30 and we went back home and unanimously decided it was bed time as we would not be getting much sleep the next night due to travel.
