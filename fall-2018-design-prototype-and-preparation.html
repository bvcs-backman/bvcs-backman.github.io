Fall 2018: Design, prototyping, and preparation

Posted on January 13, 2019 by Nathan Backman

All Startup in Seattle Stine Fellows enrolled in a 1-credit fall preparation course leading up to our travel experience. We gathered together for a 2-hour meeting one evening per week with goals to:

  1. Define the scope of the app. We held many lengthy discussion about the purpose of the app, market fit, user requirements, major and minor features, crowd-sourcing dynamics, how and where to deploy the app, how we might define success, and how to measure and encourage user engagement.
  2. Develop an early prototype. To maximize our productivity in Seattle, the team intended to create a bare-bones implementation that would allow for basic data collection and presentation. This would allow us to focus on testing, major feature additions, user interfaces, and polish in Seattle.
  3. Plan for living/travel logistics. Planning an 18-day trip required many considerations that we tackled as a group. We researched residential areas and selected a house to rent for our stay, we searched for activities to participate in and companies to visit that would allow us to experience the offerings of the region, and we discussed personal packing requirements, group equipment needs, and basic menu planning.

Throughout the semester our weekly meetings involved many discussions and coding sessions (with Luke teleconference in via Skype). The students developed proficiency in programming languages, tools, and frameworks that would be instrumental in developing our app. By the end of the semester we had a prototype in place that we were able to test on an outing to the restaurant Plaza Mexico where we recorded our first menu item reviews. This left us confident that we would really be able to hit the ground running in Seattle and to start tackling our major project goals.
