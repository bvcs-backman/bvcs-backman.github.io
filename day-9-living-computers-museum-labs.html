Day 9: Living Computers: Museum + Labs

Posted on January 20, 2019 by Nathan Backman

[Written by Michael & Drew]

Following breakfast on this morning, development continued. For the UI side, Kayle added restaurant addresses and links to said address so that users can open the with Google maps. Michael focused on bug-fixing and photo touch-ups. Drew added sorting functionality for displaying items in descending rating order and also changed some numeral values to icons on the menu listing page. Tyler and Nick reviewed people’s branches and added some star icons on the item details page.

      

On the api side, more work on photos was done by Garret. Drew dipped his toe into the api side and created an auth token generator (and put it in the wrong place initially). Following a delicious lunch of cheese filled brats (cooked by Michael), the team departed for the Living Computer Museum.

     
On arrival we explored the first floor of the museum. This floor contained a variety of new technology. From robots to video recognition to touch technology and everything in between. Tyler especially enjoyed the self-driving car/simulation, specifically trying to crash it before the AI took over.

      

Moving to the second floor, we took a tour of the famous computers through the years. The early computers being very large and very expensive. Throughout the tour, we learned a lot about both famous and infamous developers and how stealing ideas was not uncommon in the early days of tech. One of the coolest computers in the museum was a prototype Apple I that once sat on Steve Jobs’ desk and was taken from that desk by an employee after Jobs was terminated from the company.

          

After the tour, the group spread out and tried out many of these classic computers. Dr. Bezuglov struggled through the system of commands (some of which he recalled) to code out a Fibonacci number generator on an early 80’s computer. Dr. Backman and few others experienced many games from his childhood including the now famous (in the group) Spacewar. Garrett lost handily to Dr. Backman in Spacewar.

  

Nick and Tyler had a rousing game of chess on a Microsoft Idea Pad, Nick edged out the win. Michael took a trip back to the 90’s and painted with Bob Ross on the very first Wacom tablet. Kayle took to the Oregon Trail on an Apple 2e and soon learned that bullets are explosive as his wagon caught fire. Tyler also played Solitaire on Windows 95, which was particularly memorable to him. Following our time at the museum, we took a quick trip to Costco.

      

After unloading our groceries, we decided on a place to eat dinner. After a heated debate, we landed on a nearby Ethiopian restaurant. To say we were out of our element would be an understatement. After placing our orders, the manager/waiter asked us if we wanted our dishes family style or individual. In our ignorance we opted for individual. Each of our meals looked similar, the only difference being what meat and how it was prepared. Eating this meal consisted of using a sponging bread as the utensil to pick up the ingredients for consumption. This was a unique experience for all, some enjoying their meal more than others. Drew really liked his lamb which had a nice spice to it.

Following dinner, we arrived at home and continued coding. Later in the evening Luke arrived and that concluded the day.
