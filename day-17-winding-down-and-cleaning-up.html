Day 17: Winding down and cleaning up

Posted on January 30, 2019 by Nathan Backman

[Written by Dr. Backman]

Our last full day in Seattle was meant to be low-key. The morning started with breakfast and some work on the app. There were a couple of loose ends, features, and sticky notes that we wanted to wrap up before coming back to Iowa. We also re-prioritized some of the remaining features into those that would and wouldn’t appear in a first public release. We also had a few more discussions about incorporation and found a website that would help us create incorporation documents, arrange equity splits, and many of the other details involved in the process.

      

Eventually, lunch came and it was time to eat through any leftovers we happened to find. Afterwards, energy was a bit low; it had been a long trip! Games were played through a portion of the afternoon, packing started, cleaning began, and there was some general down-time had before the evening activities began.

  

At about dinner time a recent BVU CS alumna, Sarah, dropped in for a visit. She led us to a burger place (Two Doors Down) that was a short walk from the house. There, we caught up about how things had change for Sarah since she’d last met with the students; Sarah graduated last spring so these were all familiar faces.

  

Afterwards, we walked back to the house to talk a bit more with Sarah. We learned more about her career at PayPal, initial experiences in the industry, about the steps that lead her to where she is now, and many other pieces of advice.

      

Once Sarah left, final packing preparations began and many tried to get to bed earlier than usual. We were all to be ready to go at 3:45 am the next morning for a ride to the airport.
