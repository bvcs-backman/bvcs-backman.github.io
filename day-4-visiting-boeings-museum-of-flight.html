Day 4: Visiting Boeing’s Museum of Flight

Posted on January 17, 2019 by Nathan Backman

[Written by Drew & Tyler]

This day began with continued development on our application. The first major piece to be completed on this day was an error handling overhaul. This allows for a consistent error procedure from back-end to front-end. Michael began research/development on the picture-adding functionality of reviews. He had this to say about the process: “Pictures in Ionic are not documented well.”

     

In addition to some minor error fixes, the user authentication process was advanced. Users were now able to sign in and out using buttons that reflected their opposite state. In addition the username of the signed-in user now also displayed on the home screen.  These features were added to the project in some fashion by Drew, Tyler, and Nick.

     

Following morning development, the team walked to a nearby restaurant for lunch. The decision was Feed Co. Burgers less than 5 blocks away. The highlight of this meal was the Texicana burger. Featuring onion straws, coleslaw,  BBQ, and a jalapeño to boot, this was a bit hit with Dr. Backman, Tyler, and Michael. Funnily enough, later that night for dinner we also ate burgers. Not a word was mentioned about the repeat dish.

    

The highlight of the day was going to Boeing’s Museum of Flight. Here we got to see an SR-71, a helicopter from the Vietnam War, and a bunch of flight suits from various eras of war. There were many other planes within the hangar as well.

     

Next, we moved into the WWI and WWII plane hangars. There was a lot of information about dog fighting and famous pilots on both sides of the wars. The hangar was two stories tall! We saw also saw planes with a gun mounted right behind the propeller. They used a synchronization mechanism to keep the bullets from hitting the propeller. After the World Wars hangar we moved on to the space section. Here we got to go into a full-size model space shuttle used for astronaut training. We also shot up bottle rockets propelled by compressed air.

     

The final part was the last huge hangar with the Concorde, Air Force One, a Boeing 787 and 747. We were able to walk through all of these planes. The Air Force one here was the first jet-powered Air Force One plane and it was used by Dwight D. Eisenhower. We also saw a Blue Angel fighter jet. This section was very intriguing because of the diversity of the large planes and their many purposes. This day was a productive one for development and a worthwhile trip to a unique museum.
