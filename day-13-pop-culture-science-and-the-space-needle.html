Day 13: Pop culture, science, and the Space Needle

Posted on January 21, 2019 by Nathan Backman

[Written by Michael & Garrett]

At the start of today we said goodbye to Luke who had helped us tremendously throughout the life of this app and one who we’re sure will help going forward. It was awesome to work alongside a BVU CS alumnus and all of us learned a ton.

  

We departed from the house shortly after, heading over to the Space Needle, Seattle’s most famous building. The ride up the elevator to the top was fast and the view of the city from up that high was amazing. There was a building with giant spiders painted on the roof and from up that high they looked fairly realistic which we all thought was cool.

  

When we were up that high, surrounded by nothing but glass, most of us stayed on the inside and away from the edge. However, some of the fearless ones were leaning up against the glass, having a good time while they made the others uncomfortable to say the least.

  

When we came down from the Space Needle we walked a short distance over to the Pop Museum. For most of us this was the biggest surprise for the day. When it was introduced to us it was just the Pop Museum, not the Museum of Pop Culture so we assumed it was more music oriented. But to our surprise the museum was lined with movie memorabilia (from all genres, namely: Fantasy, Sci. Fi. and Horror).

 

The museum even had an indie game section where Dr. Backman, Nick, and Tyler played an intense game of Overcooked.

  

The only disappointment there had to have been the Marvel section of the museum which you had to pay an extra fee to go into. Drew was adamantly upset about this, but funneled his rage into something productive as he learned how to scratch and worked to become the next best hip-hop DJ.

      

Upon leaving the museum Tyler suggested we make a short walk over to CJ’s Eatery for lunch. Although it wasn’t special, most of us enjoyed our meals there except for Dr. Bezuglov. Most of his frustration however came from our waitress who had difficulties understanding his intentions, forgetting his soup and also misplacing his utensils.

The slight let-down for the day came next when we made our way over to the Pacific Science Center. It wasn’t a bad place to visit, and most of us had a great time playing with all the instruments and displays they had set up. However, it was clear that the targeted age group for most of the museum was quite a bit younger than all of us were. Only a few of the exhibits were adult-friendly.

  

We left the museum after having our fill and went back to the house for some down-time before Brian Broll (A BVU alumnus working at Microsoft Research) came to the house for a visit.

      

I (Michael) was especially excited when he arrived because not only did he go on to achieve a PhD in computer science, but it was heavily associated with artificial intelligence, a field that I personally find extremely interesting. I (Garrett) found his (and his colleagues’) current application of AI interesting. They were using different games to test different AIs. Notably, they were using a software called Malmo to interface with Minecraft. We talked for a while about different applications and types of AI in some of our favorite video games. Most of us in the house were blown away with Brian’s vast knowledge of Computer Science. It was clear that he was a master of the field, someone who was motivated and passionate with an endless amount of insight and tips for all of us.

      

While all of us combined probably couldn’t match his understanding of code some of us could however beat him in a game of Smash Bros. Shortly after wrapping up on Smash Bros. We noticed a pair of flies buzzing around. Dr. Backman, our expert marksman, stepped up to the challenge of getting rid of the nuisance armed only with rubber bands. He missed on the first fly a few times, mentioning the slanted ceilings. Once he hit the first fly, though, he was dialed in. On the next shot, he hit the other fly, dropping it to the floor.

Brian left and we began to wind down for the night. Some of us hopped on our laptops to solve a few problems on Kattis (a website for solving programming contest problems) and others opted to watch the movie Total Recall (the original with Arnold Schwarzenegger).  After finishing the movie, most of us decided that was just about the best time to end the day and headed off to bed.
