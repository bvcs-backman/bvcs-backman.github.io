Day 11: Adobe, Google, & Gas Works Park

Posted on January 21, 2019 by Nathan Backman

[Written by Kayle & Tyler]

As per usual, we started our day with breakfast. The main goals for the day were to visit two companies, Adobe and Google, in the Freemont neighborhood of Seattle. We walked a bit out of the way to see a statue of Lenin. The statue was huge and was brought to the USA by an American who found it in a Czechoslovakian scrapyard (with a homeless person living inside of it). It changed hands a few times in America, and is still for sale now.

        

After visiting Lenin, we backtracked to Adobe. We met with three Adobe employees, all of which were in engineering there. They were accommodating, providing us with free doughnuts and drinks. One of the first things we asked was how you know what company to stay at. One thing they mentioned was that it wasn’t uncommon for people to be at Adobe for more than 10 years (recall that Amazon’s average was around 2.5 years.) They said that over their time at Adobe, they’ve worked on a few different projects each. Stephen and Justin are now working primarily on the backend of Adobe Spark, a web application to design visual content (visual stories, memes, etc.) quickly. Anna worked more on the user-facing side of Adobe XD. It was great to learn about the workflow and the day-to-day at Adobe.

     

For lunch, we went to El Camino, a Mexican restaurant nearby. Stephen, Justin, and Anna from Adobe joined us, along with the next person we’d planned on meeting with: Brennan from Google. Many of us thought that the food was good, however the portions were pretty small.

Our next tour was at Google, led by Brennan. Here we got to see many Google buildings and lots of Google employee perks. One of which is free food, all. the. time. There were many cafeterias to eat at as well. We did lots of walking amongst all the buildings and got to see the awesome layout of their campus. Here, we actually got to see the employee workspace. Brennan said the Google buildings in Seattle are primarily used for developing Google maps and other various Google apps. Brennan also gave us a mock interview as a group. We learned that coming to an answer in a group interview is difficult. However, I (Tyler) cannot truthfully admit I would have made it into Google right then and there. I have some work to do. I (Kayle) thought that I was on the right track to answering both questions, however I wasn’t the most verbal. I didn’t really want to be wrong in front of 10 people.

After our interview at Google, we visited a troll under a bridge.

We walked to Gas Works Park, an industrial park renovated into kids’ playground.

 

We spent some time kidding around, climbing the jungle gyms and messing with equipment there.

      

Finally, we made our way down to Lake Union, where we began skipping rocks. Tyler was exceptionally good at this, and Garrett managed to skip a half-brick. Other endeavors of Garrett included picking up a cabbage that had washed up. We tried to tell him not to.

 

After making it home, we all got to work. My (Kayle’s) goal of the day was to fix our broken master branch of the app. It turned out that one word had been omitted from a pretty important part of the app, causing any addition to the data to throw an error. I also worked on moving the User Creation page to a better spot – within the sign-in page. My (Tyler’s) goal of the day was to continue the creation/development of the recommendation engine for the app. We want to have the app give the user suggestions on what to eat. We take menu items that users have ranked highly and find other similar and highly ranked menu items based on this data. Just imagine a robot waiter that recommends menu items for you to order based on menu items you have enjoyed in the past.

  

We got to do a lot today, visiting two companies, going out to eat, visiting a troll, and ending at a park throwing rocks. This was another successful day in Seattle.
