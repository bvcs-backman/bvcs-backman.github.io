Day 5: Nature and views at Rattlesnake Ridge

Posted on January 18, 2019 by Nathan Backman

[Written by Kayle & Garrett]

This was a day about nature. We all woke up, had some breakfast, and piled into the van to head out to Rattlesnake Ridge. We listened to a podcast about startups on the way out of the city. It featured, Paul Graham, one of the founders of Y Combinator which is a popular startup incubator. It was interesting to hear someone talk from the view of very successful startups.

      

Eventually, we made it to the mountain and got our bearings. Prepared with water bottles, we began the climb on a hike up the mountain. After some amount of time, we made it to the end of the trail and certain people in our group changed their minds on just how worthwhile the climb was after seeing the pot of gold at the end of the steep rainbow.

The view was stunning. I (Garrett) have no other way to state it. We hung around at the top for a while and I (Garrett) sat for a minute with my legs over the edge which was a little closer than some of our group could stomach. To be honest, they were a little more sensible than I was. After soaking in the view for 20 minutes or so, we decided it was time to head back down.

      

While on the way up, we had seen a few people jogging, and Michael & I (Garrett) decided to try jogging down the mountain. The rest of us walked the trail back, realizing just how much easier it was. I (Kayle) talked with Dr. Bezuglov most of the way, and we fell behind the others a bit. We (the jogging group) got well ahead of the rest of them. We made it about half way back down and then switched back to walking. While fun, I realized I had very wrong shoes to be doing such activity. After the other group made it back to the bottom, we all walked over to the lake and goofed off for a bit before departing.


            
On the way back home, we stopped for some food at the Riverbend Café. Little did we know walking in, that this place had some steep prices and mediocre food. I (Kayle) ordered the Rueben and got a side of French Onion soup. The sandwich was fine (though a bit poorly crafted) but the soup was downright tasteless. Ironically, the French Onion soup cost $1 more than chicken noodle. I (Garrett) ordered the Blue Plate which was basically a meatloaf sandwich. I enjoyed the sandwich but Nick, who also ordered it, did not. However, we both thought the fries that came with the sandwich were quite lackluster.

      

After finally arriving home, we got some much needed free time.  Dr. Backman made up some Honey Baked Chicken for dinner, a recipe often made in his home. That food more than made up for the poorer quality meal we had earlier. After dinner, we got to work. We had all finished up our work around 9:00, leaving us to finish the day with some free time before bed.
