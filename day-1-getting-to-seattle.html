Day 1: Getting to Seattle

Posted on January 16, 2019 by Nathan Backman

For most of us, our journey started at BVU’s Estelle Siebens Science Center. We loaded into a university van and drove to the Omaha airport. There, we met up with the rest of our team (those that lived close to Omaha), checked in for our flight, and gathered for our first team photo on the trip.

The government shutdown didn’t seem to impact our TSA lines (although Kayle got an extra dose of attention during screening).  Our flights on Southwest Airlines were on-time and pleasant. On the first flight, Drew and Tyler were seated next to a knowledgeable traveler who pointed out the many interesting geological features as we flew. Garrett and Michael, on the other hand, were excited to end up with an empty seat between them and appreciated the extra room.

   
Dinner during our layover in Phoenix, was a bit of a let-down. There were no rave reviews of the airport tacos. Our following flight was also on-time and a nice ride. We landed in Seattle greeted by a “BVU Stine Fellows” sign that lead us to the van that took us into Seattle and to our rental house.

The van was impressive, heavily lit with blue LED lights, and was blasting “crunk” music by Lil Jon as we entered. The experience was definitely one to remember! The ride into Seattle that night provided spectacular views of the downtown cityscape. We arrived at our rental house where we were greeted by a gift basked of treats by the owners who wished us well on our startup venture.

The day was long and we were happy to find ourselves in an awesome house that seemed to be a perfect place to start diving into development.
