Day 6: Crunch day

Posted on January 18, 2019 by Nathan Backman

[Written by Tyler & Drew]

     

This day consisted mainly of development to prepare for demos we planned to give in Portland the next day. On the UI end, the major pieces we worked on involved user authentication and appearances. For the review entry page, Drew added sliders and Nick changed the rating number to a set of stars. Tyler fixed a problem where users would be signed out of the app upon opening it; now users stay signed in until otherwise directed. Along with fixing several small errors, Kayle completed his app-wide alert function. This brought consistency to error messages for users.  As for the API portion of development this day, Garret took the helm (he usually does for the API). The main focus was on the development and storage of images in the database. This work consumed many days for the team and was a large hurdle that we faced.

     

For the other non-development portions of our day, we began with lunch at El Gallito in our neighborhood. This Mexican restaurant was fairly uneventful, an average lunch to be sure. As per usual, data was added to the production database. For dinner that day, we had a special dish. Dr. Bezuglov made Plov, a rice and pork dish. For all six students this was a new dish and we were interested to try. Overall it was highly regarded and we even had enough for later meals.  The evening ended with a game called Codenames. This word play based game lead to some laughs and groans a like.
