Day 12: Cruising the harbor & visiting the aquarium

Posted on January 21, 2019 by Nathan Backman

[Written by Nick & Drew]

Today was the day we started our journey on the Seattle City Pass. Before that could happen, some work was needed to be done. Development took place in the morning and evening. For the UI side solid quality of live improvements took place. Nick implemented a home button to allow users to go to the home screen from any other page. Toasts were completed on this day by Kayle. Toasts are a small notification at the bottom of the screen when the user completes an action. For example, signing in will provide the user with a toast saying “Successfully singed in.”.  Michael and Kayle continued and completed development on adding photos to reviews (the whole team rejoiced). Drew refactored the UI side of most POST requests to send user ID and an authentication token to the api and stored tokens on user sign in.

      

On the api end, Garrett and Michael worked diligently on both phots and user authentication. After completing these tasks the app was in a solid state reaching another millstone. As for the recommendation engine Tyler and Dr. Bezulglov continued development.

 

After the lunch and morning development, we departed for the water front.  On the harbor cruise we learned a lot about Seattle from our enthusiastic tour guide. It was fairly rainy, cold and windy at the start of the cruise so those who preferred the warmth didn’t stay on the top deck.

      

On tour the guide mentioned that the Space Needle could survive a 9.1 earthquake many of us found this to be quite interesting. Nick especially enjoyed the California Sea Lions lying about on various buoys and fencing. Another interesting fact was the sheer amount of grain exported by Seattle, grain being a top export of Seattle. The guide mentioned the grain was brought by rail from midwestern states.  After the cruise we walked to the nearby aquarium.

      

Throughout our time at the aquarium we saw many animals and were even allowed to touch a few. Sea anemone, star fish, and sea cucumbers were all able to be touched at the “Life on the Edge” exhibit. Tyler even touched a small crab.

      

The aquarium also featured a variety of fish both salt and fresh water. Some notable fish were the puffer fish, clown fish, sea horses, and unicorn fish. The aquarium also featured some non-fish animals including, sea lions, puffins, kill deer and oystercatchers.

  

Tyler and Nick were able to catch the feeding of the otters which was a cute display. The anticipated highlight for many was the feeding of the two octopuses. However this event was somewhat disappointing as the octopuses were slow to act(asleep for a time) and hard to see due to the rounded tanks.


         
After the aquarium we were quite hungry and made our way to Ivar’s. At first we went to the sit-down version of Ivar’s, but we checked out the menu before being seated and promptly left the restaurant. Luckily less than 50 ft away was the outdoor order-at-the-counter-while-standing version of Ivar’s. This stand had much more reasonable prices. Here the group ordered a variety of fried sea food and chips/clam chowder. Garrett especially liked his side order of fried green beans, while Drew enjoyed his bread bowl of white clam chowder. After the meal many of us walked outside the patio to the pier and fed some seagulls. Crackers and French fries were flung and often caught midair by the experienced seagulls. After Ivar’s we returned home.

Following our nightly development session, the team sat down with Luke to learn about his job and ask questions about his real world experience. Luke began by summarizing his current role as a quality assurance developer. This meant developing thousands of internal tests to test code and snuff out bugs. Luke then answered some of our questions. He spoke about advancement at tech related jobs. To summarize the higher up a developer goes the more likely they will spend time in meets and code reviews compared to actually coding.  At the conclusion of this sit down we thanked Luke for all his help throughout this process and began to wrap up for the evening.
