Day 15: Seattle Underground Tour

Posted on January 24, 2019 by Nathan Backman

[Written by Tyler & Nick]

Once again, we all woke up and had our own breakfast. We did some work in the morning on our app before making plans for the rest of the day.

      

We decided to go on the historic Seattle Underground Tour. This tour was downtown near the waterfront in Pioneer Square where Seattle was initially created.

  

Our tour guide was very energetic and knowledgeable from the start. She packed a lot of information in with humor. We all really enjoyed her. We learned how Seattle was single handedly built by David Maynard. David had many jobs and was the city’s everything. He especially helped with the construction of the city. Henry Yesler was the next guy we learned about. Henry was a con artist who was regarded as a founder of Seattle and even was elected mayor. While mayor, Henry (as a citizen) sued “himself” (the city) and as mayor settled (with himself, the citizen) before the courts could step in. Yesler (the citizen) pocketed this money. This happened twice in his first term of office! He also hired his niece as the first city employee; she was 12 years old and lived in Ohio. He pocketed that paycheck too. To add to the destruction of Seattle, Henry hired and paid a large amount of money to a company (that he owned) to build the water system to support the fire department. He spent so little on the actual infrastructure (pocketing the rest) that the water system failed 30 minutes into the Great Seattle fire that ended up destroying the city. Henry Yesler did other damage to the city infrastructure by taking similar cost-saving shortcuts that the city of Seattle is still paying for to this day – over 100 Million dollars this past year alone! Henry was a bad, dirty man.

      

Seattle had problems of its own too. It was accidentally built beneath sea-level (initial measurements were taken at low-tide) which resulted in a sewer system that would erupt in a geyser of sewage from the toilets whenever the tide would come in.

      

After the fire wiped out the city, there was an opportunity to re-build above sea level. This would take many years to accomplish and companies were impatient so they built buildings with the expectation that the second floor would eventually be at street level. Going underground was very cool to see as the current Seattle is literally built on top of these old and discarded lower levels.

  

The underground tunnels of old Seattle went everywhere it felt like and I (Tyler) was lost as soon as we turned the first corner. All the debris underground was original from the old buildings. We got to see old signs, bootlegger doors, and many spiders. In total, our tour guide said there is 160 acres of “underground Seattle”! This was an awesome tour because we got to see very genuine artifacts and the tour guide made it very entertaining instead of just a boring history tour.

  

As we finished the tour, we asked our guide for a good local restaurant and she directed us to Delicatus. It was a nice sandwich restaurant in Pioneer Square. I (Nick) got a Gypsy Rose Lee, which had a cured, thin cut salami sort of meat which tasted pretty good. I (Tyler) got the Duwamish, a sliced ham and shredded pork sandwich with a weird sauce. Once we finished our meals there, we headed back to the house where we would have down time until 4:30.

  

Working after 4:30 was a grind for Michael. He totaled EIGHTEEN commits on this day alone. Way to go Michael! It is impossible to decipher everything he worked on but a strong majority of it were bug fixes and improvements that overall, bettered our app. Thanks, Michael. Nick continued work on the search by item page and made solid progress there. Tyler and Drew worked together on refactoring the recommendation engine to better deliver results. On the API side, Garrett worked hard to fix up photos and developing the API. We all put in a lot of work in the evening hours.

  

While everyone was working on code, I (Nick) started making Lasagna around 5:00. I mixed ground beef with spaghetti sauce and layered the dish with noodles, the meat, cottage cheese, more noodle, cottage cheese again, and topped by mozzarella with parmesan sprinkled over it. I then heated it in the oven for 30 minutes, getting some work on coding in while occasionally checking its progress. They said they loved it and only scraps remained when we were done with it.

Yet again, another awesome day in Seattle.
