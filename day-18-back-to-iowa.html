Day 18: Back to Iowa

Posted on January 30, 2019 by Nathan Backman

[Written by Dr. Bezuglov]

Our last day in Seattle started early at around 3:45 AM. At 4AM we boarded a luxurious van that would take us to the airport. Our chauffeur felt obliged to enlighten us further about Seattle, Washington state, and Northwest in general. As a result of this conversation we learned that: (1) the word ‘Iowa’ does not start with a consonant letter, (2) even though the best bagels in the world are baked in New York city, Seattle follows closely, (3) underage wine students in Washington can now taste the subject.

 

We arrived to the airport and headed to the security check, where we saw TSA agents diligently performing their duties even though they have not been paid because of the gov’t shutdown. This time the security check was done without lost wallets, bags, or people and we arrived to the departure gate well ahead of time.

  

Both flights were smooth and uneventful and we arrived to Omaha airport at 1:20PM. Nick, Garrett, and Tyler met their relatives, we all took the last photos, before the group started to shrink. Those headed to Storm Lake, got into the university van, which was kindly arranged and driven by Dr. Shepherd and who quickly but safely brought us back to BVU.

  

Dr. Backman could finally relax, exhale, run “this->herd_mode = FALSE;”, and the trip had officially ended.
