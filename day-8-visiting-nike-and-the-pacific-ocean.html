Day 8: Visiting Nike and the Pacific Ocean

Posted on January 20, 2019 by Nathan Backman

[Written by Kayle & Nick]

Today was another day in the Portland area. We woke up early at the hotel (6:00 AM) so that we could get breakfast at 6:30 in the hotel and meet back up. To beat the traffic, we left around 7:00 for Nike in Beaverton. We arrived at 8:30, where we took a slide from the second floor of the parking garage down to the ground level. We walked around the Nike campus while David (a friend of Dr. Backman’s) talked to us about the perks for working there as well as the details of his job.

  

Unlike others that we had met with, David is a project manager and coordinates the efforts of many different groups to deploy Nike mobile apps globally. He gave us a different perspective on the industry, showing us that although he has a degree in Computer Science, his job doesn’t require coding daily.

      

While we walked around campus, he showed us Nike Lake, a manmade lake that is around 7 feet deep. David also told us about grand opening events that Nike held whenever a new building was opened on the campus. One included Tiger Woods visiting Nike to show off his skills. They floated a small island in the center of Nike Lake, and donated money for each ball he could land on it. In the end, he landed all 25 on the island. After roaming campus (and realizing the huge scale of it,) we went to a conference room where we continued talking about David’s job and the company as a whole.

      

After our tour of Nike, we started to head from town. We stopped at Panda Express for lunch, which made us consider what our app should do for chain restaurants. We are still undecided on this. We then left town for Cannon Beach (the beach from The Goonies). The drive was long, but when we arrived we realized that the drive was worth it.

  

The beach was really neat, the tide was low and the waves varied a lot. Because of this, some of us mistakenly walked to what we thought was the water front. It was not. A larger wave came in, causing me (Kayle) to run back, narrowly escaping the wave. Garrett was not so lucky. The waves took him, watering down his shoes until we got home 4 hours later.

   

While looking around on the beach we saw plenty of seashells and rocks that had been smoothed over by the ocean waves. We started making our way closer to the large Haystack Rock near the shore. In between it and the beach were a number of rocks and piles of sand sticking out in the water, enough that you could probably hop your way across them to the large rock. A number of us walked out onto these rocks. Every now and then a wave would come in that would cover some of the rocks with water, resulting in more of us getting wet.

      

On the way back to the car, we noticed that the high tide leaves jellyfish washed up on the beach. Some of us poked at them a bit, but we lost interest eventually, and so we went back to the car. Driving back to our rental house, we stopped to eat at Country House Diner on interstate 5. Some of us thought our food was underwhelming, though Bezuglov got ‘The Bigfoot’ which was a large chicken-fried steak that one might describe as the size of a big foot. The two of us (Kayle and Nick) were going to finish his meal since he could only finish half of it. We cut it in half, but before Kayle could take his piece from the plate Garret swooped in with the speed of a falcon and ate Kayle’s half in one bite. In Kayle’s own words: “I couldn’t believe it. The speed at which he skewered and devoured ¼ of the huge dish. I just wanted to try it.” He would go hungry another night…

After the small town diner, we continued on the journey home. The remainder of the drive was about 2 ½ hours. When we arrived, we all took a break – it had been a long day.
