A high tech travel course in the Pacific Northwest

Computer science students thrive on challenge and opportunity. This BVU computer science travel course, Startup in Seattle, provides such an environment. Our students are challenged to form a startup and produce a compelling software application while we explore employment opportunities in the Pacific Northwest by visiting a variety of companies and professionals within the tech industry. This 18 day adventure extends from January 8th to the 25th during BVU’s 2019 interim period.

Students gained enrollment into the course through a competitive application process. We received 14 applications but could only admit 6 students as Stine Fellows onto the team. Enrollment was selective because the course is funded by the School of Science Stine Endowment. Startup in Seattle is actually the second in a series of “World of Science” courses in which the Stine Endowment provides funding for the entire experience of the trip excluding a minimal $200 fee to the student. In the case of Startup in Seattle, the Stine Endowment provided $25,000 in funding to cover both travel expenses and seed funding for the students’ company. Seed funds were used to purchase equipment, tools, software licenses, and additional infrastructure to begin developing the application.

The Startup Idea

During the 2014-2015 academic year, Luke Anderson (a senior, at the time) conceived and developed a mobile application to assist restaurant-goers in determining “What’s Good?” on a restaurant menu. Reviews of menu items were to be submitted by users and the app would present the results back to those browsing the menu from the app. Luke created a working prototype and presented it as his computer science capstone project.

The idea had promise but, after graduation, Luke focused his energy into working at QCI as a Software Consultant. Years later, the opportunity to launch Startup in Seattle came about. With Luke’s permission and support (he is our alumni consultant and a remote member of our team) the Startup in Seattle Stine Fellows elected to revive the project, bring it to fruition, and deploy it.
