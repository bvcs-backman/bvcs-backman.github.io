Day 10: Visiting Amazon‚Äôs downtown campus

Posted on January 20, 2019 by Nathan Backman

[Written by Garret & Tyler]

This was the first full day our alumni member, Luke, joined us in Seattle. All of us managed breakfast on our own and we headed out to go meet with Nick for a visit at Amazon. We found a parking garage under a building near our destination and managed to hit every hanging sign at least once, sometimes twice, on our way to park the van. This was not our fault, the signs hung lower than the clearance bar at the entrance of the garage indicated.

  

Once we emerged from six stories underground and appeared in downtown at Amazon‚Äôs spheres, Nick took us to Amazon‚Äôs banana stand where they just hand out free bananas. Next, we walked down to Amazon‚Äôs Go Store where you scan yourself in, grab your items, and literally leave without ‚Äúchecking out‚Äù. This was a very new experience for us because it felt a lot like shoplifting! Not that we would know what shoplifting feels like . A few minutes later, anyone who picked something out, received an email from Amazon with the exact receipt for the items they grabbed.

  

After this fun, we went to a building to talk with Nick. Nick gave us great insight on what it is like to work at Amazon. An interesting thing we learned was that people who have been employed at Amazon for roughly two and a half years, are above the median in time with the company.

     

Once we learned all we could from Nick, we headed to the nearest Shake Shack where we met what felt like the half city in line to order a burger. While most of agreed the burgers were decent, some of us were disappointed with the quantity, especially given the price.

      
Amazon‚Äôs spheres were our next destination after Shake Shack. We did not get to go up in them because we were not Amazon employees. Instead, we got to enjoy the tourist part with fancy screens and directional sound speakers. This concluded our visit in the spheres.

     

When we arrived back at the house, we (your humble writers) played a few rounds of one of the games we brought along before splitting up to do our own thing for down time. I (Garrett) made another trip to the YMCA a few blocks away. I (Tyler) took a nap. We both thought we did what we needed to at the time. We reconvened at 4 to get back to coding. Before dinner we worked on a few things including fixing something of a memory leak and moving user creation on the UI side and making authentication tokens and cleaning things up for the API side.

      

For dinner Michael made French dip sandwiches. These were honestly fantastic; everyone at the house enjoying the masterfully crafted sandwiches. After dinner, we went back to work continuing development. I (Garrett) worked to further clean up the API, removing the current implementation of photos in preparation for a redesign. For the UI, we made some visual tweaks and touch ups to increase how easy it would be to code going forward.
